# Social Media Manager

This skill manages social media content across platforms — from post creation and hashtag research to content calendar planning and engagement analysis. It handles platform-specific formatting, optimal posting times, hashtag strategy, and audience-tailored messaging across Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram, Facebook, and Bluesky.

## When to Use This Skill

- Creating platform-specific social media posts
- Researching hashtags for reach and relevance
- Planning weekly or monthly content calendars
- Repurposing long-form content into social posts
- Optimizing post timing for engagement
- Analyzing engagement patterns and suggesting improvements
- Creating social media campaigns with connected themes
- Writing thread/series content for Twitter or LinkedIn
- Generating platform-appropriate visual descriptions

## What This Skill Does

1. **Post Creation**: Craft platform-optimized posts with proper formatting and tone
2. **Hashtag Strategy**: Research and select hashtags for reach, relevance, and discovery
3. **Content Calendars**: Plan weekly/monthly posting schedules with variety
4. **Content Repurposing**: Transform blog posts, videos, and long-form content into social posts
5. **Engagement Analysis**: Assess post performance and recommend improvements

## Process

### Step 1: Platform-Specific Post Creation

Each platform has distinct formatting, length, and tone requirements:

```markdown
## Platform Specifications

### Twitter/X
- **Length**: 280 characters (aim for 71-100 for max engagement)
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 per tweet (more reduces engagement)
- **Tone**: Conversational, punchy, opinion-friendly
- **Media**: Images get 2x engagement; video even more
- **Threads**: Number each tweet (1/7, 2/7, etc.)

### LinkedIn
- **Length**: 1,300 characters max (1,500-2,000 for articles)
- **Hashtags**: 3-5 per post
- **Tone**: Professional, value-driven, thought leadership
- **Format**: Hook line → line break → body → CTA
- **Best**: Personal stories, lessons learned, industry insights

### Instagram
- **Caption**: 2,200 characters (first 125 visible before "more")
- **Hashtags**: 5-10 in caption or first comment (up to 30 max)
- **Tone**: Visual-first, authentic, emoji-friendly
- **Reels**: 15-90 seconds, trending audio, hook in first 2 seconds
- **Stories**: Polls, questions, countdowns for engagement

### Facebook
- **Length**: 40-80 characters ideal for engagement
- **Hashtags**: 1-2 per post
- **Tone**: Community-focused, personable
- **Best**: Questions, behind-the-scenes, user-generated content

### Bluesky
- **Length**: 300 characters
- **Hashtags**: Optional; discovery is feed-based
- **Tone**: Conversational, community-driven
- **Feeds**: Post to relevant custom feeds for discovery
```

### Step 3: Hashtag Strategy

```markdown
## Hashtag Research Framework

### Category Mix (per post)
- **Branded** (1): #[YourBrand] or #[CampaignName]
- **Industry** (1-2): #[Industry] #[NicheArea]
- **Content-specific** (1-2): #[TopicOfThisPost]
- **Discovery** (1): #[TrendingOrPopularInYourSpace]

### Research Process
1. **Brainstorm seed terms** from the post topic
2. **Check platform search** for each seed — note post volume and engagement
3. **Analyze competitors** — which hashtags do similar accounts use?
4. **Balance reach and specificity**:
   - High-volume (1M+): Broad reach, low relevance (e.g., #marketing)
   - Medium-volume (10K-1M): Sweet spot for most posts (e.g., #contentmarketing)
   - Low-volume (<10K): Niche, highly relevant (e.g., #b2bsaascontent)
5. **Test and iterate** — track which hashtags drive impressions weekly

### Hashtag Rules by Platform
| Platform | Count | Placement | Strategy |
|----------|-------|-----------|----------|
| Twitter/X | 1-2 | In text | Specific > broad |
| LinkedIn | 3-5 | End of post | Industry + niche mix |
| Instagram | 5-10 | Caption or comment | Mix of volumes |
| Facebook | 1-2 | In text | Simple, brand-focused |
| Bluesky | 0-2 | Optional | Feed-based discovery |
```

```markdown
- Key stats or quotes: [List]

**LinkedIn Post**: Personal angle on the main thesis → 3 key takeaways → CTA
```


## Error Handling

- **Character limit exceeded**: Trim from the body first, preserve the hook and CTA; warn user about truncation
- **Hashtag not found**: Use broader industry terms; verify hashtags are currently active (not from abandoned campaigns)
- **Platform API unavailable**: Provide ready-to-paste content for manual posting; include all text, hashtags, and visual descriptions
- **Content calendar conflicts**: Avoid posting the same topic on the same day across platforms; stagger by 2-4 hours minimum
- **Tone mismatch**: If a post feels wrong for the platform, flag it — LinkedIn shouldn't sound like Twitter, and vice versa

## Common Pitfalls

- ❌ Same post on every platform — each platform has its own language and audience
- ❌ All promotion, no value — the 80/20 rule: 80% value, 20% promotion
- ❌ Ignoring replies — engagement is a two-way street; respond within hours, not days
- ❌ Over-automating — scheduled content still needs a human review before posting
- ❌ Hashtag stuffing — more hashtags ≠ more reach; platforms penalize overuse

## Cline Workflow Notes

1. **Install location**: Copy this skill directory to `.cline/skills/social-media-manager/` (project-level) or `~/.cline/skills/social-media-manager/` (global)
2. **Platform-first thinking**: Always specify which platform before creating content
3. **Always include a CTA**: Every social post should drive an action, even if it's just "reply with your experience"
4. **Preserve brand voice**: Ask for existing social posts to match tone and style
5. **Calendar as living document**: Update the content calendar as plans change; don't treat it as fixed
6. **Batch creation**: Create a week's worth of content at once for consistency
7. **Respect platform norms**: Don't use LinkedIn language on Twitter or Instagram casualness on LinkedIn

## Dependencies

No external dependencies required. This skill uses Cline's native capabilities with optional integration to social media APIs via other skills (twitter-post, bluesky, linkedin, Instagram API).
